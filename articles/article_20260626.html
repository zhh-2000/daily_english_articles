文章摘要

2026年4月，中国电池制造商Greater Bay Technology首次在生产线成功生产全固态电池，通过针刺、挤压、热冲击等严苛测试且无一起火。固态电池替换了传统锂电池中的易燃液态电解质，实现更快充电、更长续航和更高安全性。这一突破标志着固态电池从实验室走向量产的关键里程碑。

英文正文

In April 2026, a company most Americans had never heard of quietly crossed a line that the global auto industry had been approaching for two decades. Greater Bay Technology (GBT), a Chinese battery maker, rolled its first A-sample all-solid-state battery cells off a working production line. The cells passed needle penetration tests, extrusion tests, and thermal shock tests. Not one of them caught fire.

That last detail is not a minor point. The battery inside most electric vehicles today uses a liquid electrolyte, a flammable chemical solution that moves lithium ions between electrodes. It works well under normal conditions. But puncture it, overheat it, or charge it too fast, and that liquid can ignite. It's the reason EV fires burn hotter and longer than gasoline fires, and the reason fire departments have had to rethink their response protocols. The liquid is the problem. Solid-state batteries replace it with a solid material, which doesn't burn.

And here's the part the industry has been chasing since the early 2000s: that swap also allows faster charging and potentially longer range, because a solid electrolyte is more stable under the stress of rapid ion movement. The company's new cells are reported to support fast charging rates with minimal degradation over charge cycles, meaning they don't lose capacity as quickly as today's lithium-ion packs when charged hard and often.

GBT is not new to fast charging. In recent years, the company set what it described as a world record for EV charging speed, taking a battery from 0% to 80% in a claimed record time using its Extreme Fast Charging technology. The solid-state cells represent a step beyond that: faster, safer, and built without the flammable architecture that made fast charging a thermal management headache.

The commercial pressure behind all of this is real and measurable. In recent years, electric vehicles represented a significant and growing share of new vehicle sales globally, up sharply from a much smaller share just five years earlier. In China specifically, a majority of new vehicle sales were battery electric or plug-in hybrids. That scale creates a problem: the better lithium-ion gets, the more obvious its ceiling becomes. Range, safety, and charging speed are all constrained by the same liquid electrolyte that made the technology possible in the first place.

So why has solid-state taken this long? The chemistry wasn't the only obstacle; manufacturing was. Solid electrolytes are brittle. They crack under the mechanical stress of repeated charging and discharging. They're hard to produce at scale without defects. And defects in a solid-state cell don't degrade performance gradually; they kill it. For twenty years, every promising solid-state advance ran into the same wall: laboratory results that didn't survive contact with a factory floor.

The specific moment, a production line, working cells, no fire, is the kind of concrete milestone that has been conspicuously absent from solid-state coverage for years. Every previous announcement came with a timeline measured in years and a caveat about manufacturing readiness. This one came with test results.

Whether GBT delivers at a meaningful commercial scale within its stated timeline remains to be seen over the next 12 months. But the question has changed. It's no longer a question of whether solid-state batteries can be made. It's whether they can be made fast enough.

In February 2026, CATL and Changan unveiled the Changan Nevo A06, the world's first mass-production passenger EV running a sodium-ion pack. CATL's Naxtra cell sits at about 175 Wh/kg today, close to lithium iron phosphate (LFP), and the company is targeting LFP-level density within three years to reach around 600 km of range.

Battery prices are falling significantly. According to BloombergNEF's 2025 battery price survey, the average lithium-ion pack fell 8% to a record-low $108 per kWh in 2025, now 93% cheaper than in 2010. The result is that electric vehicles are becoming more affordable for consumers worldwide.

长难句解析

句子1

原文: "In April 2026, a company most Americans had never heard of quietly crossed a line that the global auto industry had been approaching for two decades."

语法考点: 定语从句（that引导）、过去完成时

解析: "most Americans had never heard of"是省略关系代词的定语从句，修饰"company"。后面的"that the global auto industry had been approaching for two decades"是that引导的定语从句，修饰"line"。主句使用一般过去时"crossed"，而定语从句使用过去完成时"had been approaching"，表示在主句动作之前已经持续进行的状态。

翻译: 2026年4月，一家大多数美国人从未听说过的公司悄悄跨越了全球汽车行业二十年来一直接近的那条线。

句子2

原文: "The battery inside most electric vehicles today uses a liquid electrolyte, a flammable chemical solution that moves lithium ions between electrodes."

语法考点: 同位语、定语从句

解析: "a flammable chemical solution that moves lithium ions between electrodes"是"liquid electrolyte"的同位语，对其进行解释说明。同位语中的"that moves lithium ions between electrodes"是定语从句，修饰"solution"。全句主干结构为：主语battery + 调语uses + 宾语liquid electrolyte。

翻译: 如今大多数电动汽车内部的电池使用液态电解质，一种在电极之间移动锂离子的易燃化学溶液。

句子3

原文: "And here's the part the industry has been chasing since the early 2000s: that swap also allows faster charging and potentially longer range."

语法考点: 祁使句、定语从句、宾语从句

解析: "here's the part"是祁使句结构。"the industry has been chasing since the early 2000s"是省略关系代词that的定语从句，修饰"part"。冒号后的内容是对"the part"的解释说明，其中"that swap also allows..."是宾语从句结构，"swap"是主语，"allows"是谓语，"faster charging and potentially longer range"是并列宾语。

翻译: 这正是行业自2000年代初以来一直追求的部分：这种替换也允许更快充电和潜在的更长续航。

句子4

原文: "For twenty years, every promising solid-state advance ran into the same wall: laboratory results that didn't survive contact with a factory floor."

语法考点: 时间状语、定语从句、隐喻表达

解析: "For twenty years"是时间状语。主句主干为：主语every promising solid-state advance + 谓语ran into + 宾语the same wall。冒号后的"laboratory results that didn't survive contact with a factory floor"是对"the same wall"的解释说明，其中"that didn't survive contact with a factory floor"是定语从句，修饰"results"。注意"ran into the same wall"是隐喻表达，指遇到同样的障碍。

翻译: 二十年来，每一项有前景的固态突破都撞上了同一堵墙：无法经受工厂车间考验的实验室结果。

讨论问题

Question 1

Why are solid-state batteries considered safer than traditional lithium-ion batteries?

中文翻译: 为什么固态电池被认为比传统锂离子电池更安全？

答题提示: 可以从以下几点回答：①传统电池使用易燃的液态电解质，穿刺、过热或快充时可能起火；②固态电池用固态材料替换液态电解质，不燃烧；③固态电池通过了针刺、挤压、热冲击等严苛测试无一起火；④EV火灾比汽油火灾燃烧更热更久，固态电池可避免这一问题。

Question 2

What challenges have prevented solid-state batteries from reaching production for twenty years?

中文翻译: 什么挑战使固态电池二十年来未能实现量产？

答题提示: 可以从以下几点回答：①固态电解质脆性大，在反复充放电的机械应力下容易破裂；②大规模生产难以避免缺陷；③固态电池中的缺陷不会逐渐降低性能，而是直接导致电池失效；④实验室结果无法在工厂车间存活。
