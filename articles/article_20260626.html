文章摘要

NASA的毅力号火星车在杰泽罗陨石坑发现了一种独特的岩石，具有"豹纹斑点"特征，含有与地球上微生物生命相关的矿物。结合近期发现的有机碳化合物和创纪录的镍元素浓度，这些证据表明早期火星可能具备支持微生物生命的条件。科学家称之为"潜在生物标志"，需要将样本带回地球进一步确认。

英文正文

NASA has not confirmed life on Mars — but the evidence is the strongest it has ever been. In September 2025, scientists published a Nature study describing "leopard spot" mineral patterns in a Jezero Crater rock that match the chemical fingerprints microbes leave behind on Earth. Then, in April 2026, a follow-up study revealed record-breaking nickel concentrations — up to 1.1% by weight, the highest ever measured in Martian bedrock — sitting right next to organic carbon in the same ancient river valley.

In July 2024, NASA's Perseverance rover rolled up to an arrowhead-shaped rock nicknamed Cheyava Falls in the Bright Angel formation — a set of light-toned outcrops along Neretva Vallis, an ancient river valley about a quarter-mile wide that once carried water into Jezero Crater. The rover drilled a core sample from the rock, named Sapphire Canyon, and after more than a year of scrutiny, NASA announced that it remains the mission's best candidate for evidence of ancient microbial life.

What made this rock special were its "leopard spots" — millimeter-scale whitish splotches ringed by dark material, scattered through organic-carbon-bearing mudstone. Chemical analysis with the rover's PIXL and SHERLOC instruments showed the spots are enriched in two minerals: vivianite, an iron phosphate, and greigite, an iron sulfide. On Earth, these minerals routinely form as by-products when microbes "breathe" iron and sulfur compounds instead of oxygen.

The peer-reviewed study, published in the journal Nature, calls the features a potential biosignature — a structure or substance that could have a biological origin but needs more study before scientists can rule out non-living explanations. The minerals sit inside reaction fronts — zones where chemical change swept through the mud after it was deposited.

On Earth, this kind of chemistry is the energy economy of mud-dwelling microbes. Billions of years ago, organisms similar to Earth's earliest anaerobic bacteria could have fueled themselves with exactly these reactions. Crucially, the team found no evidence the rock was ever heated to high temperatures or soaked in acid — the two most common non-biological ways to create similar minerals.

The story gained a fresh chapter in April 2026. A team led by researchers at Purdue University published a study in Nature Communications analyzing 126 sedimentary rocks in Neretva Vallis. They detected nickel in 32 rocks at concentrations reaching 1.1% of the rock's weight, the highest abundance ever observed in Martian bedrock.

Why does nickel matter in the search for life? On Earth, nickel is an essential ingredient in enzymes used by many of the most ancient lineages of archaea and bacteria. These microbes need nickel for energy generation, carbon fixation, and breaking down organic matter. Finding abundant nickel directly adjacent to organic carbon and reduced sulfur means that if anything ever lived in this valley, the metal toolkit its metabolism required was sitting right there.

The broader scientific community is approaching these discoveries with a mix of intrigue and caution. While the evidence is compelling, the path to definitively declaring the presence of past alien life is complex and fraught with uncertainty. NASA has categorized these findings as "potential biosignatures" — substances or structures that might originate from life but require further investigation to confirm.

The evidence for ancient life on Mars has never been stronger; the verdict, for now, stays open. What separates the current evidence from past false alarms is convergence — not one anomaly but a stack of independent findings in one place: a long-lived lake, preserved organic carbon, redox minerals, and bioavailable nickel at record concentrations.

长难句解析

句子1

原文: "In September 2025, scientists published a Nature study describing 'leopard spot' mineral patterns in a Jezero Crater rock that match the chemical fingerprints microbes leave behind on Earth."

语法考点: 定语从句（that引导）

解析: 本句包含一个that引导的定语从句，修饰先行词"mineral patterns"。定语从句中还嵌套了一个省略了关系代词的定语从句修饰"chemical fingerprints"。全句主干结构为：主语scientists + 谓语published + 宾语a Nature study，后面的describing为现在分词作后置定语修饰study。

翻译: 2025年9月，科学家发表了一项Nature研究，描述了杰泽罗陨石坑岩石中的"豹纹斑点"矿物模式，这些模式与微生物在地球上留下的化学指纹相匹配。

句子2

原文: "What made this rock special were its 'leopard spots' — millimeter-scale whitish splotches ringed by dark material, scattered through organic-carbon-bearing mudstone."

语法考点: 主语从句、非谓语动词（过去分词作后置定语）

解析: 句首的"What made this rock special"是主语从句，作为全句的主语。谓语动词为were，宾语为"leopard spots"。破折号后的"ringed by dark material"和"scattered through organic-carbon-bearing mudstone"都是过去分词短语作后置定语。

翻译: 使这块岩石特别的是它的"豹纹斑点"——被深色物质环绕的毫米级白色斑块，散布在含有机碳的泥岩中。

句子3

原文: "The peer-reviewed study, published in the journal Nature, calls the features a potential biosignature — a structure or substance that could have a biological origin but needs more study."

语法考点: 非谓语动词（过去分词作后置定语）、定语从句、宾语补足语

解析: "published in the journal Nature"是过去分词短语作后置定语，修饰study。主干结构为：主语study + 谓语calls + 宾语features + 宾语补足语a potential biosignature。破折号后that引导的定语从句修饰"structure or substance"。

翻译: 这项发表在Nature期刊上的同行评审研究将这些特征称为潜在生物标志——一种可能具有生物起源但需要更多研究才能排除非生命解释的结构或物质。

句子4

原文: "What separates the current evidence from past false alarms is convergence — not one anomaly but a stack of independent findings in one place."

语法考点: 主语从句、并列结构

解析: "What separates the current evidence from past false alarms"是主语从句。谓语动词为is，宾语为convergence。破折号后使用not...but...并列结构进行对比说明。

翻译: 将当前证据与过去的虚假警报区分开来的是综合性——不是单一的异常现象，而是在一处地点堆叠的多项独立发现。

讨论问题

Question 1

What makes the "leopard spots" in the Cheyava Falls rock significant for the search for life on Mars?

中文翻译: 切亚瓦瀑布岩石中的"豹纹斑点"对火星生命探索有什么重要意义？

答题提示: 可以从以下几点回答：①斑点含有vivianite和greigite矿物，这些矿物在地球上通常与微生物生命相关；②这些矿物是微生物"呼吸"铁和硫化合物而非氧气的副产物；③岩石没有被高温或酸性液体处理过，排除了最常见的非生物形成方式。

Question 2

Why does the discovery of nickel in Neretva Vallis matter for understanding potential ancient Martian life?

中文翻译: 为什么在涅雷特瓦峡谷发现镍对理解潜在的古代火星生命很重要？

答题提示: 可以从以下几点回答：①镍是地球上最古老的细菌和古细菌酶的重要组成部分；②微生物需要镍进行能量生成、碳固定和分解有机物；③发现镍直接与有机碳和还原硫相邻，意味着如果这里曾有生命，其代谢所需的金属工具就在那里。

Question 3

Why do scientists describe the findings as "potential biosignatures" rather than confirmed evidence of life?

中文翻译: 为什么科学家将这些发现描述为"潜在生物标志"而非确认的生命证据？

答题提示: 可以从以下几点回答：①非生物化学过程也可以产生类似的矿物模式；②火星车上的仪器无法进行同位素比率质谱分析和电子显微镜检查等精确分析；③非凡的声称需要非凡的证据，确认外星生命需要将样本带回地球进行实验室分析。
