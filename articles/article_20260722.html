Digital Technology Brings Traditional Culture to Life

📅 2026年7月22日 📊 文化类·数字化传承 ⏱️ 阅读时长: 8分钟 📖 词数: 920

📝 文章摘要

2026年暑期，VR虚拟现实、数字交互、实景AI等前沿技术加速落地中国文旅场景，为传统文化解锁全新消费体验。南京德基艺术博物馆沉浸式古画展厅日均客流超5000人次，江宁织造博物馆红楼梦智能机器人实现情感交互，全国博物馆数量突破7188家。数字技术不仅提升观众体验，更重塑文化产业全链条，推动传统文化在数字时代焕发新生机。

As summer 2026 unfolds, cultural venues across China are witnessing an extraordinary transformation. At Nanjing Deji Art Museum, visitors line up to experience an immersive ancient painting exhibition where the 30 distinctive scenes and 533 character figures from the classic "Jinling Tu" scroll come alive through cutting-edge digital technology. The museum now attracts over 5,000 visitors daily, a testament to the powerful combination of traditional art and modern innovation.

This phenomenon is part of a broader trend sweeping through China's cultural landscape. According to the National Bureau of Statistics, China's cultural industry revenue exceeded 20 trillion yuan in 2025, with digital-driven new cultural formats playing an increasingly leading role. In the first quarter of 2026 alone, 16 industry categories with distinct new-format characteristics generated over 1.6 trillion yuan in revenue, an 11.2% year-on-year increase.

When Classic Literature Meets Artificial Intelligence

Along the historic Yangtze Road in Nanjing — a 1,800-meter stretch that holds 1,800 years of history — another technological marvel awaits. At the Jiangning Imperial Weaving Museum, two special "intelligent employees" greet visitors with warmth and wit. These are AI-powered robots modeled after classic characters from "Dream of the Red Chamber," one of China's Four Great Classical Novels.

What makes these robots remarkable is their ability to capture and respond to visitors' emotions. Equipped with artificial intelligence, facial recognition, and intelligent interaction technology, they can detect subtle changes in visitors' expressions and tone of voice, engaging in real-time conversations that feel surprisingly natural and personal.

"We structured various versions of 'Dream of the Red Chamber' texts so that AI could precisely understand the relationships between characters, events, places, and objects," explained Chen Hong, director of the Digital Operations Department at Nanjing Cultural Tourism Group. "This ensures that what the robots say preserves each character's personality." Behind this achievement lies a massive dataset — 41.83 billion text corpus entries and 68 million sample sentences — forming the foundation of what they call the "Changgan Large Model."

Virtual Reality: A Window to the Past

At the Ming Xiaoling Museum, middle school students on study tours are discovering history in an entirely new way. Wearing VR headsets, they watch as the 600-year-old stone beasts along the sacred way suddenly "come alive." The solemn rituals of the Ming Dynasty royal court and historical scenes materialize before their eyes in vivid, three-dimensional detail.

This shift from passive observation to active participation represents a fundamental change in how museums engage their audiences. Data from cultural tourism platforms reveals that from January to May 2026, bookings for museum-type attractions rose over 40% year-on-year, while orders for interactive experiences with guided tours grew by 62%.

A Nationwide Cultural Renaissance

The numbers tell an impressive story. By the end of 2025, China had 7,188 registered museums nationwide — achieving the goal of one museum per 200,000 people. Post-90s and post-80s visitors now account for over 60% of museum-goers, and "traveling for museums" has become a new lifestyle choice among young people.

Museums are no longer solemn places for quiet observation. Night openings, study workshops, and creative cultural products have transformed visitors from passive viewers into active cultural participants. The Shanghai Museum's ancient Egyptian civilization exhibition, which integrated VR experiences with role-playing games, generated 760 million yuan in revenue and drove over 35 billion yuan in related consumption across Shanghai — setting an industry record.

Technology as a Catalyst for Creativity

In Hangzhou's Yichuang Town, a hub for creative industries, a team is developing a cultural tourism project using naked-eye 3D video technology. The impact of AI on their workflow has been transformative. "With AI technology development, we can save more than 50% of production time," said Chen Yi, founder of Time Coordinates Imaging Technology.

This town alone has attracted over 3,000 cultural and creative enterprises, forming a complete industrial chain from animation creation and game development to post-production and digital distribution, with annual revenue exceeding 10 billion yuan. Across Sichuan Province, intangible cultural heritage that once depended solely on oral transmission and paper records now thrives through high-definition digital capture, cloud archiving, VR immersion, and AI restoration — what experts call a "digital archive space" for heritage protection.

Looking Forward: A Living Legacy

The transformation extends beyond major cities. County-level museums are becoming cultural landmarks through distinctive exhibitions and creative products. In Chongqing and Sichuan, museums generated over 500 million yuan in creative product sales in 2025, while institutions like the Palace Museum and Sanxingdui Museum each surpassed 100 million yuan in annual creative product revenue.

As digital technology continues to evolve, it offers unprecedented opportunities to make traditional culture not just preserved, but experienced. The goal is no longer simply to display artifacts behind glass, but to let them speak, move, and connect with new generations. In this digital age, China's rich cultural heritage is finding its voice once again.

📖 长难句解析

句子一

At Nanjing Deji Art Museum, visitors line up to experience an immersive ancient painting exhibition where the 30 distinctive scenes and 533 character figures from the classic "Jinling Tu" scroll come alive through cutting-edge digital technology.

结构分析：主句为"visitors line up to experience an immersive...exhibition"。where引导定语从句修饰exhibition，从句中主语为"the 30 distinctive scenes and 533 character figures"，谓语为come alive，through...表示方式。
翻译：在南京德基艺术博物馆，游客排队体验沉浸式古画展览，通过前沿数字技术，经典《金陵图》画卷中的30个特色场景和533个人物形象栩栩如生地呈现在眼前。

句子二

What makes these robots remarkable is their ability to capture and respond to visitors' emotions, equipped with artificial intelligence, facial recognition, and intelligent interaction technology.

结构分析：What引导主语从句"What makes these robots remarkable"作主语，谓语为is，表语为their ability...。equipped with...为过去分词短语作状语，补充说明机器人配备的技术。
翻译：这些机器人的独特之处在于它们能够捕捉并回应游客的情感，它们配备了人工智能、面部识别和智能交互技术。

句子三

The Shanghai Museum's ancient Egyptian civilization exhibition, which integrated VR experiences with role-playing games, generated 760 million yuan in revenue and drove over 35 billion yuan in related consumption across Shanghai, setting an industry record.

结构分析：主句主语为"The Shanghai Museum's...exhibition"，which引导非限制性定语从句修饰exhibition。谓语为generated和drove（并列），setting为现在分词作结果状语。
翻译：上海博物馆的古埃及文明展览将VR体验与角色扮演游戏相结合，创造了7.6亿元收入，带动上海全域相关消费超350亿元，创下行业纪录。

句子四

As digital technology continues to evolve, it offers unprecedented opportunities to make traditional culture not just preserved, but experienced, connecting new generations with their cultural roots in ways never before possible.

结构分析：As引导时间状语从句。主句主语为it，谓语为offers，宾语为opportunities。to make...为不定式短语作定语修饰opportunities，其中包含"not just...but..."结构。connecting为现在分词作伴随状语。
翻译：随着数字技术的不断发展，它为传统文化提供了前所未有的机遇，不仅使其得到保护，更能让人们亲身体验，以前所未有的方式将新一代与他们的文化根源连接起来。
